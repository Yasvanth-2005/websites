About

What is Diwali/Deepavali?

The D-Day has finally come. Many of us are eagerly waiting for this day. What id the speciality of this day? It's diwali. Again why do we celebrate diwali? Is bursting firecrackers really necessary? Let's learn more about this festival

The festival gets its name from the row (avali) of clay lamps (deepa) that Indians light outside their homes to symbolize the inner light that protects from spiritual darkness. is a time to understand the meaning of real prosperity that 'brings light to our lives and sparkles to our eyes'

Why do we celebrate diwali?

The day of Diwali is associated with many historical events.The main reasons are :-

In nothern India,they celebrate the story of king Rama's return to Ayodhya after he defeated Ravana by lightning rows of clay lamps.

Southern India celebrates it as the day that Lord krishna defeated the demon Narakasura with the help of satyabhama. Hence we often hear from our elders that diwali is also celebrated as victory of good over evil.

In western india the festival marks the day that Lord vishnu, the Preserver (one of the main gods of Hindu trinity) sent the demon King bali to rule the nether world.

One of the most important aspects of Diwali is the worship of Maha Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity

Specality of Diwali

True Lamps to Light :-

Each year we adorn our homes with brightly lit earthen lamps. We must remember to not only light the lamp in our home, but also in our hearts. We must light the lamps of truth, purity, divinity and peace in our lives

A Fresh Start :-

On Diwali, many people begin a new chapter of their life. Look at the year that went by. How many people did we hurt? How many did we heal? How many times did we lose our temper/ How many times did we give more than we recieved/ Let us truly examine ourselves carefully

Pros and cons of Diwali

The main thing is we get to spend our time with our families. This festival is really beautiful. With the preparation of the festive food to decorating the house to bursting crackers. Its all exciting and fun. Lightning diyas and lanterns and putting up fairy lights. Bursting crackers releases smoke which kills the mosquitos and thus reduces the diseases spread by them

But it has many cons. It affects environment. Crackers enhance the specialty of this festival. Beautiful to look at. But, the way it affects the environment is really painful to look at. I'm sure lord Ram didn't want to be welcomed with loud bangs which aren't peaceful at all. but we really need to reduce the amount of crackers we burst. it causes major air pollution and the smoke is bad for people who have lung problems suc as asthama etc.

Deepavali itself says "the row of clay lamps" We should lit the lamps and make our home colourfull instead of bursting crackers and polluting air. Let us all take a step forward to create awarness among others about about the cons of bursting crackers.

As the saying goes "every journey starts with a single step" same as "Every change starts with a single person" I hope you, me, we all can be that person. This diwalli let us lit our home with clay lamps instead of bursting crackers. Let you be the one who change this world.
